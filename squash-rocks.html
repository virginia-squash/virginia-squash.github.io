Our Mission

Squash Rocks pairs squash with academic support, mentorship, and life skills. We meet kids where they are and stick with them year-round, season after season, helping them grow into confident athletes, students, and leaders.

The Program

On Court

Weekly coaching sessions teach the fundamentals of squash. Kids develop discipline, focus, and the kind of resilience only sport can teach.

In the Classroom

Sessions often pair court time with academic check-ins and homework support, reinforcing what kids are doing at school and reminding them that effort off the court counts too.

Around the Table

Many sessions wrap with a shared meal. For some kids, that's the most reliable dinner of the week, a chance to refuel, talk, and decompress together as a team.

In the Community

Mentorship from volunteer coaches and Richmond squash players. Social events and lasting relationships that extend well beyond the court.

Anna Julia Cooper School

Squash Rocks is based at the Anna Julia Cooper School in Richmond's East End, a tuition-free independent school serving students from underserved communities. The partnership has anchored the program since day one.

The school provides the daily structure. Squash Rocks provides the racquets, the courts, the coaching, and a community of mentors who show up week after week.

"Squash, of course, is the means and the vehicle, but the heart of it is the relationships he creates, loving and caring for them week after month after year. I'd like to have 100 partners like Russ and Squash Rocks."

Michael Maruca Head of School, Anna Julia Cooper School
